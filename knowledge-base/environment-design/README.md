# Environment Design

**Overview**: How physical, digital, and architectural environments shape human behavior, cognition, and wellbeing.

## Core Principle

> "We shape our buildings; thereafter they shape us" - Winston Churchill

Environments encode values, influence behavior, and affect cognitive performance. Good design emerges from understanding recurring patterns in how humans interact with space, not imposing arbitrary aesthetic preferences.

## Key Insights

1. **Physical environment affects performance by up to 20%**
2. **Biophilic design** (plants, nature views) significantly improves wellbeing and productivity
3. **Pattern recognition** reveals universal solutions to recurring design problems
4. **Design encodes values**: Architecture embeds power structures into physical space
5. **Universal design** often emerges from designing for specific constraints

## Christopher Alexander's Pattern Approach

1. **Recognize forces** in environments (privacy needs, social spaces, movement patterns)
2. **Identify patterns** of behavior and space that resolve these forces
3. **Combine patterns** adaptively for specific circumstances
4. **Iterate** based on lived experience

## Quick Start

1. **Audit workspace**: Light quality, noise levels, visual clutter, ergonomics
2. **Add nature**: Plants, natural light, views of nature
3. **Zone spaces**: Dedicated areas for focus, collaboration, restoration
4. **Reduce friction**: Place frequently-used items within easy reach
5. **Iterate weekly**: Small adjustments compound
